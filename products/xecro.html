FAQ

Xecro questions, answered.

How does Xecro protect the buyer?▾
The buyer's funds are locked in a secure vault the moment they're deposited. The seller cannot access them until the buyer explicitly approves the delivery. If the seller fails to deliver, the buyer can raise a dispute and receive a refund.
How does Xecro protect the seller?▾
The seller can see that the funds are confirmed and locked before they begin work. Once delivery is approved, funds are released instantly to their wallet — no delays, no chasing payment.
What happens if there's a dispute?▾
Either party can raise a dispute. The Nolubz Xecro team reviews all submitted evidence — including the original deal terms, delivery proof, and communication — and mediates a fair resolution.
What is the difference between Xecro Basic and Xecro Pro?▾
Xecro Basic handles single-step deals — one payment, one delivery, one release. Xecro Pro handles milestone deals — the total is split into stages, each with its own locked payment that releases independently as milestones are approved.
Can Xecro be used for cross-border deals?▾
Yes. Xecro works across all 40+ countries supported by Nolubz. A buyer in Toronto can safely transact with a seller in Nairobi — the same protection applies regardless of location.
What currency are Xecro deals settled in?▾
All Xecro deals are settled in NOLZ — 1 NOLZ = 1 USD. This gives both parties a stable, predictable value throughout the deal regardless of local currency fluctuations.
